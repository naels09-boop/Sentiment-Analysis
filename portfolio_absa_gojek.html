01

The problem

Gojek is an Indonesian super-app with over 100 million downloads, spanning ride-hailing, food delivery, and payments. A single review usually mixes opinions about several services at once — so an overall star rating or one-label sentiment score hides which part of the experience users actually love or hate.

This project frames that gap as an information-systems problem: convert unstructured, code-mixed Indonesian reviews into a structured signal that product and operations teams can act on — sentiment resolved to the level of each individual service aspect.

03

LLM-assisted labeling

Hand-labeling 51,098 aspect–sentiment pairs by hand was infeasible. Instead, I used Qwen2.5-0.5B-Instruct as a local annotation engine — small enough to run offline and cheaply, instruction-tuned enough to follow a strict aspect–sentiment schema.

Crucially, the LLM is an assistant, not an oracle. Every design decision treated its output as a draft to be audited, not ground truth.

Human validation on a stratified 300-sample set

Aspect identification held up well (90%), while sentiment polarity was harder (61.33%) — a concrete, honest finding: LLMs recognize what a review is about more reliably than how the user feels, especially on ambiguous, code-mixed text.

Model comparison & the efficiency finding

I benchmarked a monolingual Indonesian model (IndoBERT) against a multilingual one (mDeBERTa-v3) on the same held-out test set, evaluating with macro-averaged metrics so minority classes count equally.

ModelAccuracyMacro F1Weighted F1Params
IndoBERT · selected0.96770.96560.9678124.4M
mDeBERTa-v30.89410.88860.8955278.8M
The bigger model did not win.

mDeBERTa-v3 carries 2.24× more parameters, yet IndoBERT scored ~7.7 macro-F1 points higher on this Indonesian-dominant corpus. The takeaway I carried into the writeup: language-fit and evaluation rigor matter more than raw model size — and the smaller model is also cheaper to serve.

I also checked generalization explicitly: train-vs-test macro-F1 gaps were small for both models (0.0268 for IndoBERT, 0.0110 for mDeBERTa-v3), well under a 0.05 overfitting threshold, and justified the chosen epoch count with an explicit 1–10 epoch sweep rather than a fixed guess.

05

Beyond classification: Semantic Network Analysis

Classification tells you a sentiment label; it doesn't tell you the story. I built a keyword co-occurrence network over positive and negative reviews to surface the concepts shaping opinion.

The word driver sits at the center of the network, tightly linked to terms about waiting, difficulty, and cancellations — pinpointing where operational attention pays off. ABSA says which aspect; SNA explains why.

06

Deployment

The best model ships as an interactive Streamlit dashboard: pull live reviews by app ID, run aspect-level inference, view per-aspect sentiment summaries and auto-generated conclusions, explore the SNA network, and export results to CSV. The deployment passed all 13 black-box test scenarios covering data fetch, inference, summaries, ABSA–SNA integration, and export.

What this project demonstrates

NLP & Modeling

  • Fine-tuning transformer encoders (IndoBERT, mDeBERTa-v3) for aspect-level classification
  • LLM-assisted data annotation with a small instruction-tuned model, run locally
  • Monolingual vs multilingual model evaluation on low-resource, code-mixed text
